# v2.4.2 — Need-and-Upside Draft Policy (design)

> Segment **v2.4.2** of phase v2.4 (`v2.4-policy` off `v2`). Refactors the draft policy in
> `frontend/lib/draftAI.ts` from a multiplicative heuristic into the additive objective
> specified in `docs/modeling/DRAFT_LOGIC.md`. The v2.4.1 backtest harness is the safety net.

## Why a refactor, not a rewrite

`draftAI.ts` is already well past "best VORP by need": it has positional-run detection,
need-weighted demand, K/DST deferral, and a rich bench model (handcuff / buried-upside /
contingent / bye-coverage). The gap is **form**, not ambition:

- The spec defines an **additive** objective (`marginal_starter_value + bench_value −
  penalties`); the code is **multiplicative** (`base_VOR × (1+need) × run × adp × …`).
- Multiplicative stacking hides magnitudes and compounds silently (three 1.2× bonuses →
  1.73×). The additive form forces each signal to declare "how many points is this worth,"
  which is what makes the 2.4.3 ablations meaningful (each term toggles independently).
- The one genuinely missing piece is **true marginal starting-lineup value**; today's
  `positionDemand` only counts open slots as a proxy.

## Objective & units

Everything is expressed in **projected fantasy points** in the active league's scoring (the
common currency that makes addition valid; VOR/boom are available on `p.value`).

```
pick_score(cand) =  max( marginalStarterValue(cand), benchValue(cand) )
                  − overfillPenalty(cand)

hard constraint:  K ≤ 1, DST ≤ 1  until the final 2 rounds  (a gate, not a multiplier)
```

A candidate is credited for the **better** of "improves my starters now" or "best bench
asset," never both at once — `benchValue` only wins once the starting slots it is eligible
for are full.

## Term A — `marginalStarterValue` (replacement-aware)

```
marg(cand) = proj(cand)
           − expectedReplacementAtNextTurn(pos, pool, picksUntilNext, runs)
```

- `proj(cand)` — the candidate's projection in the active league's scoring.
- `expectedReplacementAtNextTurn` — the projection of the player at this position you could
  **realistically still get at your next pick**, found by walking the available pool down by
  the expected number of picks gone before your next turn. **Positional runs feed this**: a
  hot position depletes faster → replacement drops → marginal rises.

This deliberately **folds the spec's separate `scarcity_opportunity_cost` into Term A**.
"What I lose by not taking the run position now" *is* "how much better this player is than
what survives to my next turn." Keeping them as two terms would count scarcity twice and
over-draft running positions.

FLEX / superflex-OP falls out for free: replacement is computed at the position the candidate
would actually occupy in the *optimal* lineup (via the existing `fillRoster` eligibility), so
a superflex QB sees a thin replacement pool (high marginal) while a 3rd RB sees a deep one
(low marginal) — no hand-tuned slot weights.

## Term B — `benchValue` (the focus — the requirement's "weakest part")

```
benchValue(cand) = E[starts](cand) × valueWhenStarted(cand) × availability(cand)

E[starts] = byeCover(cand, myStarters)                     // weeks a starter is on bye
          + injuryCover(cand, coveredStarter, isHandcuff)  // P(starter misses)×games,
                                                           //   amplified for same-team handcuffs
          + ceilingWeeks(cand, marginalStarter)            // boom weeks that outscore a weak starter

valueWhenStarted = boom-weighted projection (ceiling, not mean — what a bench piece realizes)
availability     = health / role prior (default ~0.9; lower for a buried or uncertain role)
```

- **byeCover** — a bench player who can fill a starter's bye-week slot earns those starts.
- **injuryCover** — `P(starter misses games) × games`, **amplified for same-team handcuffs**:
  a handcuff is *negatively correlated* with its starter's availability — it starts precisely
  when your starter is out — so its expected starts are conditioned on that injury.
- **ceilingWeeks** — the upside term: weeks the candidate's boom outscores a marginal starter.
  This is what mechanically makes a high-ceiling WR4 beat a 2nd kicker.

## Hard constraints

- **K/DEF cap** — owning ≥1 K (or ≥1 DST) makes a second one **ineligible** until
  `round > totalRounds − 2`. A gate, not a multiplier, so no freak-VOR kicker can override it.
  *(Decision: last 2 rounds, reusing the existing `lateDraft` boundary.)*
- **Positional caps** — `overfillPenalty` ramps once a position passes
  `startSlots(pos) + reasonableDepth(pos)` (RB/WR deeper than QB/TE in superflex). Prevents a
  5th-RB stack. With replacement-aware marginal this is mostly automatic; the penalty is the
  explicit backstop.

## Availability data note

We have limited injury data on the player record (`metadata.depth_chart_order`,
`search_rank`; no reliable live injury designation in the snapshot). `availability` therefore
defaults to a **~0.9 prior**, lowered for a buried/uncertain role, and is a tunable parameter
rather than a fitted per-player number. If a richer injury feed lands later it slots in here.

## Integration & single source of truth

- All logic stays in `draftAI.ts` (`scoreBoard` / `pickForTeam`), so the **live boards**
  (`frontend/scripts/simulate.ts`) and the **backtest sim** (`frontend/scripts/draftSim.ts`)
  receive it identically through `frontend/lib/snakeDraft.ts` — the D7 "one policy"
  guarantee, untouched.
- Every coefficient (replacement horizon, run-depletion factor, the three bench-source
  weights, boom weight, availability prior, per-position injury rates, cap threshold, overfill
  curve) lives in a **named, exported parameter object** so 2.4.3 can grid/ablate without
  code surgery.

## Testing (TDD — written before implementation)

Unit tests per pure term, plus behavioral acceptance taken straight from the spec:

- 3rd RB on a full lineup → low `marginalStarterValue`; needed superflex QB → high.
- High-ceiling WR4 → `benchValue` > a 2nd K/DST.
- Same-team handcuff → `injuryCover` premium over a generic backup.
- **No K/DST drafted before the final 2 rounds** across a full simulated draft (the headline
  regression — the original complaint).
- Determinism preserved: a fixed seed still fully determines a draft (the `mulberry32` path).
